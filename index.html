Condensed Matter Physics (CMP) Lab

Department of Physics, Jashore University of Science and Technology

Overview

The CMP Lab is focused on **computational condensed matter physics**. We use cutting-edge theoretical methods, including **Density Functional Theory (DFT)**, to investigate the fundamental properties of materials. Our research supports critical advancements in **energy storage**, **spintronics**, and **optoelectronics**. We aim to design novel materials with specific electronic, magnetic, and optical properties, contributing to both foundational physics and practical technological applications.

Highlights

Our research is structured around several key themes:

  • **Spintronic Materials:** Investigating materials for next-generation data storage and processing, focusing on magnetic properties and spin-dependent transport.
  • **Energy Storage:** Designing and simulating novel materials for batteries and supercapacitors with improved efficiency and longevity.
  • **Optoelectronic Devices:** Exploring materials with unique optical and electronic properties for applications in solar cells, LEDs, and photodetectors.
  • **Topological Matter:** Studying materials with robust quantum states for potential applications in fault-tolerant quantum computing.
